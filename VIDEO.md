# Plan AI - Low-Budget Promotional Video Guide

This guide is designed for a low-budget, high-impact founder-led video. The format is a casual "Talking Head" (you with a coffee cup) transitioning into a rapid-fire screen share.

## 🎥 Filming Strategy & Setup

### Equipment
*   **Camera:** Your smartphone (iPhone/Android). Clean the lens! Use the rear camera (not the selfie camera) for better quality.
*   **Audio:** A quiet room is crucial. If you don't have an external mic, place the phone as close to you as comfortably possible. (Alternatively, record audio separately on your computer while filming, and sync later).
*   **Lighting:** Face a window. Natural, diffused sunlight is the best free lighting. Do not sit with the window behind you (you will be a shadow).
*   **Screen Recording:** Use QuickTime Player on Mac or OBS Studio. Keep the screen clean (hide your dock, close unrelated tabs).

### The "Coffee Cup" Vibe
The goal is to feel like a relatable founder having a chat with another professional. It should feel authentic, not over-produced. The coffee cup gives your hands something to do and sets a relaxed tone.

## 📝 Video Script & Structure (Target length: 60-90 seconds)

### Part 1: The Hook (Talking Head)
*Visual: You sitting casually, holding a coffee cup, looking directly at the lens.*

**You:** "Every week, I was spending hours sitting in meetings, and then *another* hour just trying to organize my scattered notes, figure out who was doing what, and put it into Jira."
*(Take a quick sip of coffee)*
**You:** "So, my team and I built Plan AI to completely automate that."

### Part 2: The Pitch (Talking Head)
**You:** "Plan AI isn't just another transcription tool. It's a context-aware ecosystem for your team. You feed it your project's context, and it acts as an intelligent partner during and after your meetings. And because we built it with a 'Bring Your Own Key' architecture, it's ridiculously cheap to run."

### Part 3: The Demo (Screen Recording)
*Visual: Cut to a screen recording. Talk over the footage. Keep the pace fast.*

**Voiceover:** "Here’s how it works in the real world. First, I create a Project and upload our specific context—like technical docs or Jira tickets."
*Visual: Briefly show uploading a document or setting up a 'Project Context' in the Web Dashboard.*

**Voiceover:** "Now, when I jump into a Zoom call, I just start the Desktop Recorder. But here's the killer feature..."
*Visual: Show the desktop recorder starting.*

**Voiceover:** "...while the meeting is happening, I can use the Live Chat to ask questions against our real project context on the fly."
*Visual: Show the Desktop Recorder's Live Chat panel. Type a quick question like "What was the deadline for this?" and show the AI answering based on the context.*

**Voiceover:** "When the meeting is done, Plan AI doesn't just give me a generic transcript. It generates a highly accurate summary tailored exactly to our project..."
*Visual: Scroll through a beautifully formatted Doc in the web app.*

**Voiceover:** "...it instantly pulls out action items and pushes them to your backlog..."
*Visual: Show the task extraction or Jira integration feature.*

**Voiceover:** "...and you can manage your whole team's API costs right here in the settings, down to the exact token, so there are zero surprise bills."
*Visual: Show the Workspace Settings page with the new 'Blueberry Tokens' visual limit.*

### Part 4: Call to Action (Talking Head)
*Visual: Cut back to you with the coffee cup.*

**You:** "Stop taking notes and start actually participating in your meetings with an AI that actually knows what your project is about. Check it out—link is in the description."

## ✂️ Editing Tips (Free/Low-Cost)
*   Use **CapCut** (Free on desktop and mobile) to edit.
*   **Zoom In:** During the screen recording phase, actively zoom in on the parts of the screen you are talking about (e.g., zoom in on the 'Desktop Recorder' button, then pan to the generated 'Doc'). Don't just leave a static wide shot of your screen—people watch on mobile!
*   **Captions:** Use CapCut's auto-captions feature. Bold, dynamic captions are essential since many people watch videos on LinkedIn/Twitter with the sound off.
